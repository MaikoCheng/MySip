Why should you drink water?

As we age, our bodily water reserves shrink. The reduction is about 20 to 30% from baby to adulthood. Therefore, sufficient fluid intake is more important for elderly than it is for kids.

How much should you drink?

Collected data has shown that the amount people of the age of 65 or older drink, varies a lot. It can be anywhere from 311 to 2390 mL a day. The recommended daily amount of fluid intake is 1600 mL for females and 2000 mL for males. Your own recommended intake can also be calculated using our tool.

What should you drink?

Although the quantity of fluid intake is important, we should not forget about the quality of the drink.
Certain drinks will have a negative impact on your health, while others can positively contribute to a better physical and mental state.
For example, the consumption of carbonated soft drinks leads to a higher daily energy intake. This can be either positive or negative, depending on your current dietary needs. Additionally, most elderly are at risk for a dietary deficiency in calcium intake. Calcium is an important nutrient for the human body and sufficient intake can reduce the risk of osteoporosis, fractures and diabetes. Recommendations on how much calcium should be consumed vary slightly.

Should I drink alcohol?

Drinking any alcoholic beverage has a negative effect on your level of hydration. Furthermore, alcohol worsens many pre-existing conditions and can lead to new health problems. It is advisable not to drink alcohol. Drinking alcohol while using the MySip might give a false outcome on your level of hydration.

You should always contact your caretaker or general practitioner to get specific advice on this topic.
